# product-management

## 1. Product Discovery & Validation

- Structure assumption-testing plans: identify leap-of-faith assumptions, design fastest experiments to retire risk
- Evaluate discovery methods: customer interviews, painted door tests, Wizard of Oz, concierge, prototype testing — and when each is appropriate
- Critique PRDs and specs for feature-team thinking vs. empowered-team thinking
- Assess whether a team is doing "discovery theater" (going through motions without actually reducing risk)

## 2. PR/FAQ & Working Backwards

- Write or critique PR/FAQs: the internal press release, customer quotes, FAQ (internal and external)
- Evaluate whether a PR/FAQ actually works backwards from the customer or is a solution looking for a press release
- Design the narrative structure for product decisions — 6-page memos, not slide decks
- Identify when a PR/FAQ reveals the idea isn't ready (the most valuable outcome)

## 3. Strategy & Market Analysis

- Apply aggregation theory, platform economics, and network effects analysis to competitive positioning
- Evaluate moat durability: network effects, switching costs, economies of scale, brand, regulatory capture
- Assess marketplace dynamics: Rochet-Tirole two-sided market theory, liquidity, take rates, disintermediation risk
- Identify whether a company is in a product-led, sales-led, or platform-led growth model — and whether that's right for their market

## 4. Metrics Architecture

- Design input/output metric frameworks: distinguish controllable inputs from lagging outcomes
- Identify vanity metrics and propose replacements
- Structure WBR (Weekly Business Review) dashboards
- Define "good enough" measurement when perfect attribution isn't feasible
- Call out Goodhart's Law violations ("you're optimizing the metric, not the outcome")

## 5. Team & Org Design

- Diagnose feature team vs. empowered team dynamics from descriptions of how work gets done
- Recommend team topology changes: platform teams, experience teams, enabling teams
- Evaluate whether a product org has the right ratio of discovery to delivery
- Assess product leader effectiveness: are they coaching or just approving?
- Design hiring bar-raiser mechanisms

## 6. Go-to-Market & Growth

- Evaluate GTM fit: is the distribution strategy coherent with the product model?
- Assess pricing and packaging against willingness-to-pay research (not competitor copying)
- Identify growth loops and their sustainability
- Distinguish sustainable growth from "buying growth" through unsustainable spend
